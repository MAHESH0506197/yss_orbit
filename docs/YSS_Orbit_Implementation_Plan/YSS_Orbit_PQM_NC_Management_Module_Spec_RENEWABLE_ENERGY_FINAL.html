1. Module Purpose & Objectives

1.0 Sector Context

PQM is built for the Quality function of a renewable energy EPC (Engineering, Procurement & Construction) organization executing three asset types, each with materially different inspection points, trades, and failure modes:

  • Solar — Ground-Mounted Plants: civil/foundation works, module mounting structure (MMS) erection, module installation, DC cabling and string work, inverter/transformer installation, AC yard and evacuation, SCADA/communication, fencing and site civil works.
  • Solar — Rooftop Installations: roof structural assessment, mounting rail/clamp fixing, waterproofing at penetration points, module installation, DC/AC balance of system (BoS), net-metering interconnection.
  • Wind Power Projects: foundation (raft/pile) works, tower erection (sectional), nacelle and hub installation, blade installation and torque checks, WTG (Wind Turbine Generator) electrical and control systems, cabling and pooling substation works, met mast installation.

The module’s category taxonomy, checklist references, and case study throughout this document (Section 6, Section 4) are written against this sector — not generic residential/commercial building construction.

1.1 Business Goal

PQM gives the Quality function inside a renewable energy EPC organization a single system of record for identifying, assigning, rectifying, verifying, and closing quality defects (“Non-Conformances” / NCs) discovered during solar and wind project execution — across civil, mechanical, and electrical scopes — with full accountability and an audit trail strong enough to survive a client, lender (IFC/lender’s independent engineer), or regulatory audit, not just an internal report.

1.2 Problems It Solves

  • No standardized way to log/track quality issues today across civil, MMS, electrical (DC/AC), and WTG erection trades (WhatsApp, Excel, email — none traceable or reportable).
  • No SLA visibility — issues missed, closure timelines unmeasured, especially where weather windows (monsoon, high wind-speed days) are tight and rework is costly.
  • No enforced approval chain — issues closed without proper verification, a real risk on safety-critical items like torque-checked structural bolting or DC earthing.
  • No analytics on recurring root causes, worst-performing projects/EPC contractors, or quality trends across a multi-project solar/wind portfolio.
  • No audit trail for compliance, lender’s independent engineer (LIE), or client (developer/IPP) audits at Commercial Operation Date (COD) handover.

1.3 Expected Outcomes

  • 100% of NCs raised, tracked, and closed inside the system — zero offline tracking — across civil, MMS, electrical, and WTG erection scopes.
  • Measurable average closure time per project/engineer/EPC contractor.
  • Full audit trail for internal QA, lender’s independent engineer, and client/regulatory audits at handover/COD.
  • Real-time dashboard for Quality Head / Regional Managers across a multi-project solar and wind portfolio.
  • Per-tenant configurability (categories, severities, approval chain length, SLA days) so each EPC organization can tune the module to its own solar-only, wind-only, or mixed portfolio.

2. Module Scope

2.1 In Scope (Phase 1)

NC creation → review → assignment → rectification → verification → closure workflow; configurable multi-level approval chain (1–4 approvers); attachments with versioning and authenticity metadata; email + in-app notifications; full audit trail; dashboard + KPI reporting; export (Excel/PDF/CSV); BU-scoped RBAC reusing YSS Orbit’s existing engine; duplicate detection/merge; recurring-issue linking; EPC contractor backcharge flagging; safety-critical fast-track (relevant to working-at-height on WTG towers/rooftops and live DC/AC electrical work); SLA extension requests (relevant to monsoon/high-wind-speed weather stand-downs); mid-workflow reassignment; legacy Excel data migration; offline capture & sync (relevant to remote solar parks and wind projects with poor connectivity); external client/lender’s-independent-engineer read-only access.

2.2 Out of Scope (Phase 1) — Deferred

Native offline-first mobile app; AI-based root-cause suggestion (Phase 2, Section 21); full EPC contractor self-service portal with external login (data fields reserved, login deferred); drawing markup/annotation tool; real-time finance/ERP integration for backcharges (report-based handoff only in Phase 1); direct SCADA/O&M monitoring-platform integration (module records installation-phase NCs, not post-COD O&M defects, in Phase 1).

2.3 Future Roadmap

Phase 2 — AI insights, EPC contractor portal, native mobile app. Phase 3 — predictive SLA-breach alerts, auto-checklist generation from drawings (GA drawings, SLDs), integration with post-COD O&M/SCADA defect tracking. Phase 4 — cross-tenant benchmarking (anonymized) as an advisory add-on across a portfolio of solar and wind assets.

3. Multi-Tenancy & Organizational Model

Note on terminology: earlier drafts of this spec modeled Site as a separate level below Project. For a renewable EPC portfolio, a Project is the site — a solar park or wind project complex is one physical location, not a parent with multiple child locations. This edition collapses that into a single level: Project is the atomic location unit. Finer-grained location detail within a project (a specific block, row, string, or WTG number) is captured as NC-level fields (Section 6), not as a separate hierarchy entity or database table. There is no core_site table and no site_id anywhere in this schema.

Tenant (Organization)
 └── Business Unit (BU)
      └── Project
           └── NC records

Every NC row carries organization_id and business_unit_id even though project_id already implies them — this is required, not redundant. It lets RBAC and row-level filtering happen at the outermost tenant boundary without joining through Project on every query, matching the BU-isolation pattern already used in your Organization/IAM modules.

All querysets are scoped server-side by the authenticated user’s accessible BU list. Client-supplied organization_id/business_unit_id on write requests must never be trusted — always resolve from the user’s role assignment. Cross-BU visibility (e.g., Org-level Quality Head) is a permission (pqm.view_all_bu), not a data-model exception.

4. Real-World Case Study — Full Lifecycle Walkthrough

Read this before building anything — it exercises almost every rule in this spec in one continuous, realistic example. Use it as your primary end-to-end QA test script (see Section 23).

Project: SunCrest Solar Park — 50 MWp Ground-Mounted (Kurnool, Andhra Pradesh) Tenant: Org = “Helios Renewables EPC Pvt Ltd”, BU = “Solar EPC — South Region”

Day 0 — 06 Jul 2026, 9:40 AM Site Quality Engineer Ravi Kumar finds reactive torque test readings on 6 module mounting structure (MMS) foundation piles in Block D, Row 14, recorded below the specified 5 kN·m minimum per the structural Design Basis Report (DBR-2024-Rev2).

  • New NC: Category = Civil & Foundation — Module Mounting Structure (MMS), Severity = Major, Priority = High (SLA default 5 days → target 11 Jul). Location = Block D, Row 14. Attaches 3 before-photos of the torque test readings.
  • Validation on Submit: photo EXIF timestamp within last 24 hours and GPS within the project’s registered geofence — passes. NC Number generated: NC-HRE-2026-04821.
  • Notifications: Site Incharge (Prasad), Site Quality Incharge (Meena), Project Incharge (Suresh).

Day 0, 2:15 PM — Review The civil supervisor disputes the finding. Prasad clicks Return for Correction: “Please attach the torque wrench calibration certificate and geotechnical test log before resubmission.” → Status: Draft.

Day 1, 10:00 AM Ravi attaches the torque wrench calibration certificate and geotechnical test log, resubmits. Prasad Approves, assigns Contractor = Suryodaya Structural Works (MMS erection sub-contractor), Responsible Engineer = Anil. → Status: Assigned.

Day 2–4 Anil’s team re-drives and re-tests the 6 piles to spec. Day 4: uploads 4 after-photos of the corrected torque readings, Root Cause (“Sub-contractor crew used an incorrect torque wrench calibration setting for this pile diameter, producing under-torqued readings”), Corrective Action, Preventive Action (“Add torque wrench calibration verification to the daily pre-work checklist, project-wide”). Clicks Request Closure.

  • Gate check: root cause present ✅, ≥1 after-photo ✅ → Status: Verification Pending, current_approval_level = 1.

Day 5 — Verification Chain (3 levels, per this tenant’s config)

  1. Meena (Site Quality Incharge) — Approves. 2. Prasad (Site Incharge) — Approves. 3. Suresh (Quality Head) — Approves. → Status: Closed, actual_closure_date = 2026-07-11 (SLA met on target; first_time_right = false due to the review-stage rework, but sla_breached = false).

Day 40 — Reopen Differential settlement is observed on an adjacent pile foundation in the same row during a later inspection. Site Quality Engineer Divya reopens NC-HRE-2026-04821 instead of raising a new one: “Adjacent pile foundation showing differential settlement in the same row, possible recurrence — linking for root-cause trend analysis.”

  • Status → Assigned, reopen_count → 1, full verification chain re-triggered from level 1, and a pqm_status_history row records the two lifecycles as one chained NC rather than a silent duplicate.

What this proves out: rejection/rework path, EXIF/GPS evidence validation, contractor + engineer assignment, gate-checked closure, multi-level verification, SLA math with a rework cycle, and reopen-with-audit-trail — all in one script.

4.1 Supplementary Examples — Rooftop and Wind (for cross-asset-type QA coverage)

The core NC workflow (Sections 5, 12) is identical across all three asset types; only the category, checklist reference, and project-level roles change. Use these two short examples alongside Section 4’s full walkthrough when testing rooftop and wind scenarios specifically:

Rooftop solar example: During a commercial rooftop installation (200 kWp, warehouse roof), a Quality Inspector raises an NC for a mounting rail clamp fixed without the specified EPDM waterproofing gasket at a roof-penetration point — Category = Rooftop Structural & Waterproofing, Severity = Major (roof penetration defects are a common source of post-COD leak claims), Priority = High, SLA = 3 days given the client’s occupied-building constraint. The NC references the roof structural assessment report and the waterproofing detail drawing. Closure requires an after-photo of the corrected gasket plus a water-ponding test result before Request Closure is enabled.

Wind power example: During WTG (Wind Turbine Generator) erection, a Site Quality Engineer raises a Safety-Critical NC (is_safety_critical = true, Section 20.4) for a blade root bolt torque reading below the OEM-specified value on WTG-14 — Category = WTG Erection — Blade, Severity = Major. The safety-critical flag auto-sets a 24-hour SLA and fires an immediate parallel notification to the Safety Officer role, independent of the normal review chain, since an under-torqued blade bolt is a fall-of-load/structural risk. Closure requires the OEM torque wrench calibration certificate and a re-torque record referencing the WTG’s serial number.

5. Business Process Flow

5.1 End-to-End Workflow

Daily inspection → deviation found → Raise NC (Draft)
   → Submit → notify Site Incharge / Site Quality Incharge / Project Incharge
   → Site Incharge Review
        ├── Reject/Return → back to Draft (reviewer comment attached)
        └── Approve → Assign responsible engineer → Status: Assigned
   → Corrective work → Status: In Progress
   → After-photos + root cause + Request Closure (gate-checked) → Verification Pending
   → Verification Chain (configurable, default 3 levels: Site Quality Incharge → Site Incharge → Quality Head)
        ├── Any level Rejects/Rework → back to Assigned
        └── All levels Approve → Closed
   → (optional) Reopen → back to Assigned, full chain re-triggered

5.2 Escalation Flow

If an NC stays in Assigned/In Progress past its target closure date:

  • Day 0 (due date): reminder to Assigned Engineer + Site Incharge.
  • Day +1: escalate to Site Incharge + Project Incharge.
  • Day +3: escalate to Quality Head.
  • Day +7: flagged “Critical Overdue” on dashboard, weekly digest to Org Admin. Escalation offsets are configurable per Organization (pqm_escalation_config, Section 8.9) since SLA tolerance differs per client contract.

5.3 Weather / Force-Majeure SLA Extensions

Real sites lose days to monsoon rain (civil/foundation works) and to high wind-speed days that halt crane lifts for tower, nacelle, and blade erection on wind projects; holding an engineer to an SLA breach caused by 3 days of continuous weather stand-down pollutes your compliance analytics unfairly. Assigned Engineer or Site Incharge can request an extension (/extension-request/), requiring approval from the next level up before target_closure_date changes. The original target date is never overwritten — both are stored so SLA-compliance reporting can still show whether a breach occurred before the extension was granted.

6.1 Field-level Business Rules

  • Asset Type is set at the Project level (Section 3) and inherited by every NC raised against that project — it drives which Category/Subcategory list, checklist library, and default severity mapping are shown in the NC creation form (Section 11.3). A project cannot mix asset types; a solar park and a wind project sharing the same Business Unit are still modeled as separate Projects.
  • Root Cause: optional on Draft/Submit, mandatory gate before Request Closure — otherwise closure-time and root-cause analytics fill with nulls.
  • Before photos: enforce ≥1 attachment at Submit — an NC without photographic evidence is not audit-defensible, and is often required by the lender’s independent engineer (LIE) at COD handover.
  • After photos: enforce ≥1 attachment before Request Closure is enabled.
  • Target Closure Date: must be ≥ Raised Date; default SLA by Priority (Critical=2 days, High=5, Medium=10, Low=20), configurable per tenant; Safety-Critical NCs auto-override to 24 hours regardless of Priority — relevant here to working-at-height on WTG towers/blades and live DC/AC electrical work.
  • NC Number: generated inside a DB transaction with a per-organization/year sequence counter using SELECT ... FOR UPDATE (never COUNT(*)+1, which breaks under concurrent creation). Live and legacy-migrated records use separate numbering series (LIVE vs LEGACY) so provenance is always visible and the two never collide.

9.2 Key Business Rules Enforced in services/

  • Status transitions go only through nc_service.transition_status(nc, target_status, user), validated against the state machine (Section 12) — reject anything not in the allowed map regardless of what the request claims.
  • approval_service.record_decision() is the only path that advances current_approval_level, and it re-checks the deciding user’s role/permission at decision time, not just at assignment time.
  • numbering_service.generate_nc_number() wraps the counter increment in select_for_update() inside transaction.atomic(), keyed by (organization_id, year, series).
  • Closure-gate validation (root cause required, ≥1 after-photo, all approval steps Approved) lives in validators.py, called before any transition to Closed.
  • duplicate_service.find_similar_ncs() runs a same-project + same-category + overlapping-location + 48-hour-window check on submit; merge_nc() reassigns child attachments/comments and marks the source NC Merged.
  • geofence_service.validate_photo_location() runs at attachment upload, comparing EXIF GPS against pqm_project_geofence, setting gps_within_geofence (soft flag, not a hard block).
  • migration_service.import_legacy_batch() validates rows against relaxed Phase-1 gates with an explicit import_override audit flag where historical data is incomplete, and produces a row-count reconciliation report (source vs loaded vs rejected).

20. Practical Field & Operations Considerations

These are the things that don’t show up in a clean textbook workflow but will show up within the first month of real usage.

20.1 Duplicate NC Detection

Multiple engineers inspecting the same project independently will occasionally raise the same issue twice. On Submit, a similarity check (same project + category + overlapping location + within a rolling 48-hour window) flags a possible duplicate as a soft warning (not a hard block, since it’s sometimes genuinely a distinct issue). Confirmed duplicates are merged via /merge/: the source NC’s attachments/comments are appended to the primary NC’s history (never deleted), and the source becomes Merged (terminal, read-only) with merged_into_nc_id set.

20.2 Recurring Issue / Repeat-Offender Linking

Distinct from duplicates — the same type of issue recurring at a different location/time (e.g., under-torqued blade root bolts recurring across multiple WTGs erected by the same crew, or DC earthing resistance failures recurring across multiple combiner boxes on the same feeder) is a root-cause signal, not a duplicate. linked_nc_id groups a family of related NCs; the Repeat Issue Rate dashboard KPI is often the single most useful metric for EPC contractor performance reviews.

20.3 Contractor Accountability & Backcharge Tracking

Quality NCs frequently carry a commercial consequence (cost deducted from a subcontractor’s bill for rework). backcharge_amount + backcharge_status (Not Applicable/Proposed/Contractor Disputed/Agreed/Deducted) are kept intentionally simple — just enough for Quality to flag a cost consequence, with Commercial/Finance picking it up downstream via the Backcharge Summary report rather than a real-time integration in Phase 1.

20.4 Safety-Critical Fast-Track

A safety-critical finding (under-torqued blade root bolts, missing edge protection on a WTG platform, exposed live DC conductors) bypasses the normal SLA-by-priority default: is_safety_critical = true auto-sets a 24-hour SLA, fires an immediate parallel notification to the Safety Officer role, and always surfaces on a dedicated dashboard tile regardless of other filters.

20.5 Weather / Force-Majeure SLA Extensions

See Section 5.3 — extension requests require next-level approval before target_closure_date changes, and the original date is preserved for audit purposes.

20.6 Mid-Workflow Reassignment

Engineers resign, transfer, or go on leave constantly on real projects. /reassign/ (Site Incharge/Project Incharge, any pre-closure status, reason required) logs a distinct Reassigned event so the audit trail clearly shows who actually performed the corrective work, even if it wasn’t the original assignee.

20.7 Offline Capture & Sync

Basements, remote sites, and tower-crane zones frequently have no signal. Draft NC creation should cache in IndexedDB (not localStorage) with photos held as blobs, syncing automatically on reconnect. The server accepts a client_captured_at timestamp distinct from created_at (server receipt time) — a large gap is expected and not itself suspicious, but a captured time in the future relative to server time should be rejected.

20.8 Photo Authenticity for Audit Defensibility

Per attachment, capture photo_gps_lat/lng, photo_captured_at (EXIF), and photo_exif_hash. Validate GPS against the project’s registered geofence (pqm_project_geofence, default 500m radius) at upload — flag, don’t block, since a valid reference photo (e.g., a fabrication-yard sample) is occasionally taken off-site. New uploads always create a new version row; existing attachment files are never overwritten in place.

20.9 Legacy Data Migration (Excel → PQM)

Most tenants will have 1–3 years of NCs in Excel that need to migrate in without corrupting numbering or audit integrity. /import/legacy/ (Admin-only) accepts a mapped CSV/XLSX upload; every imported record gets a permanent legacy_reference and is_migrated = true flag, and uses the separate LEGACY numbering series so historical and live numbers never collide. Run through the same validators where feasible, with an explicit import_override audit flag where historical data is incomplete — apply the same ETL rigor as any data-migration-assurance engagement: row-count reconciliation (source vs loaded vs rejected), checksum/spot-check sampling, and a migration exception report before go-live sign-off.

20.10 External Client / Auditor Read-Only Access

For projects needing end-client or third-party auditor visibility: the External Client Auditor role is scoped to specific Project(s) (not BU-wide), read-only, export-enabled, and cannot see internal pqm_comment threads (only formal fields + approval history) unless explicitly toggled per project. This is a permission profile (scope_type = 'project_list'), not a new table — it reuses the existing RBAC engine.

22. Enterprise Standards & Non-Functional Requirements

  • Multi-tenant isolation: every query filtered by organization_id at the ORM manager level (custom manager, not per-view filtering) to make tenant-leak bugs structurally harder to introduce.
  • BU isolation: consistent with existing BU-scoped RBAC.
  • Performance: index plan (Section 8) supports the two hottest queries — “my open NCs” and “overdue NCs” — target under 200ms at 100k-row tenant scale; paginate all list endpoints (default 25, max 100).
  • Security: file upload validation (type/size limits, virus-scan hook placeholder), signed URLs for attachment access (no public bucket URLs), rate-limiting on write endpoints.
  • Data retention: soft-delete only; per-tenant retention policy for closed NCs configurable (never purge, or purge after N years per contract).
  • Backward compatibility: status enum and approval-step model support adding a 4th/5th approval level later without a migration on pqm_non_conformance itself — this is why approval steps live in a child table, not fixed columns.

Appendix A. RBAC — Full Specification

This appendix is the single canonical source for PQM’s RBAC — codenames, hierarchy, seeding, and field-level visibility. Sections 7, 13, 3, and 9.2 describe RBAC conceptually; this appendix is what a developer should actually implement against.

A.1 Full Permission Codename List

Every permission below is a discrete, independently-assignable codename in the format pqm.<action>_<object>. There is no implicit bundling — a role has exactly the codenames it’s been given, nothing more (see A.2 for why inheritance is intentionally NOT used).

Codename Meaning
pqm.view_nc View NC list/detail within one’s scope
pqm.view_all_bu View across all BUs in the organization (overrides default single-BU scope)
pqm.create_nc Create a new NC
pqm.edit_nc Edit an NC while in Draft/Returned
pqm.submit_nc Draft → Submitted
pqm.review_nc Approve/Reject/Return at the review stage
pqm.assign_nc Assign or reassign the responsible engineer
pqm.update_nc_progress Start work, log progress, request an SLA extension
pqm.request_closure Submit an NC for verification (triggers the closure gate check)
pqm.verify_nc Approve/Reject/Rework at a verification-chain level
pqm.reopen_nc Reopen a Closed NC
pqm.merge_nc Merge a duplicate NC into a primary NC
pqm.link_nc Link an NC into a recurring-issue family
pqm.comment_nc Post to the internal comment thread
pqm.view_comments View the internal comment thread (separable from view_nc — see A.4)
pqm.upload_attachment Upload photos/documents to an NC
pqm.view_audit View status history / approval history / full timeline
pqm.view_dashboard View KPI tiles and charts within one’s scope
pqm.export_report Export NC Register / reports (Excel/PDF/CSV)
pqm.view_backcharge View/edit backcharge_amount and backcharge_status fields
pqm.manage_config Manage categories, contractors, escalation config
pqm.run_legacy_import Execute /import/legacy/
pqm.view_safety_critical See the Safety-Critical dashboard tile and flag (org/project-wide, bypasses normal BU scope for this one tile only)

Rule: any new action added to the module later gets its own codename — never overload an existing one (e.g., don’t make merge_nc also imply link_nc just because they’re similar actions; a tenant may legitimately want to allow one without the other).

A.2 Role Hierarchy — Explicit Decision: No Automatic Inheritance

A common mistake in RBAC design is assuming “Quality Head” automatically has everything “Site Incharge” has, because it sounds senior. This module does not use inheritance. Reasons:

  • Seniority in a reporting-line sense (Quality Head manages Site Incharges) is not the same as functional scope (Quality Head may deliberately be excluded from day-to-day assign_nc actions in some tenants, to keep assignment authority local to the site).
  • Inheritance chains become unauditable at scale — six months in, nobody can explain why a role has a permission because “it inherited it from three levels up.”
  • YSS Orbit’s existing core RBAC engine (used elsewhere in Organization/IAM) already treats roles as flat, explicit permission sets — PQM must follow the same pattern for consistency, not introduce a second paradigm just for this module.

What this means practically: the default seed roles (A.3) come pre-populated with a sensible set of codenames per Section 13’s matrix, but an Org Admin can freely add or remove any codename from any role afterward — including making a Quality Head unable to assign_nc if that tenant’s process requires it, without that decision cascading anywhere else.

If a tenant genuinely wants hierarchy-like behavior (e.g., “give the Quality Head role everything Site Incharge has, plus these three more”), that’s achieved by cloning the Site Incharge role as a starting point and adding codenames — a one-time explicit action, not a standing inheritance relationship. This “clone as starting point” pattern should be a first-class action in the Role management UI, not just a database trick.

A.3 Default Role Seeding Mechanism

  • Seed roles are shipped as a fixture file (pqm/fixtures/default_roles.json) containing the 14 roles from Section 7 with their default codename sets pre-populated per the Section 13 matrix.
  • Seeding is triggered once per new Organization, as a step in the existing Organization-onboarding flow (org_onboarding_service.provision_defaults(org) — extend this existing hook rather than adding a separate PQM-specific onboarding trigger, so PQM roles are provisioned in the same transaction as the org’s other default roles).
  • Seeded roles are fully editable copies, not references to a shared template — once created for an Organization, editing that org’s “Site Incharge” role never affects any other tenant’s “Site Incharge” role. This is critical: a shared/linked template would silently break tenant isolation the moment one client asks for a role tweak.
  • A is_system_default boolean flag on the seeded role lets the UI show “(default)” next to unmodified roles and warn an admin before they edit one (“this will only affect your organization”), purely as a UX safety net — it has no functional effect on permission resolution.
  • Re-running seeding (e.g., after a module upgrade adds a new default role like Safety Officer to existing tenants) must be additive and idempotent — insert only roles that don’t already exist for that org, never overwrite an admin’s existing customizations to a role with the same name.

A.4 Field-Level Visibility (beyond object-level view_nc)

Object-level pqm.view_nc is not sufficient on its own — some fields need to be hidden even from users who can see the NC itself. Implement as a serializer-level field filter, not by creating separate near-duplicate serializers per role:

Field(s) Visible to Hidden from
backcharge_amount, backcharge_status Roles with pqm.view_backcharge (typically Site Incharge+, Quality Head, Org Admin) Site Engineer, Quality Inspector, External Client Auditor (commercial data, not a field-execution concern)
pqm_comment thread Roles with pqm.view_comments External Client Auditor by default (toggle-able per project — see 20.10); Safety Officer (not part of the day-to-day discussion)
legacy_reference, is_migrated Auditor, Org Admin Hidden from general list views for all other roles (visible only in NC Detail’s audit tab) — it’s provenance metadata, not something a Site Engineer needs cluttering their daily list
Photo GPS/EXIF raw metadata (photo_gps_lat/lng, photo_exif_hash) Auditor, Org Admin, Quality Head Shown to others only as a computed badge (“✓ Location verified” / “⚠ Outside geofence”), never the raw coordinates — raw GPS of a project location is sensitive and unnecessary for most roles to see directly
root_cause_description (before the Request Closure gate) Assigned Engineer, Site Incharge+ Not shown to Raised-By-only viewers as “required” until the gate actually requires it, to avoid confusing early-stage viewers

Implementation pattern: one NCDetailSerializer with a get_fields() override that pops field keys based on request.user’s resolved codename set — this keeps one serializer as the source of truth instead of drifting copies per role.

A.5 Scope Resolution Order (how a request’s visible data is actually computed)

For every PQM request, scope is resolved in this fixed order — implement this as a single reusable queryset method (PQMBaseQuerySet.scoped_for(user)) rather than repeating logic per view:

  1. Is the user a Platform Admin? → No default PQM data access (must be explicitly granted pqm.view_all_bu per org if they need support access).
  2. Does the user’s role have pqm.view_all_bu? → Scope = all BUs in their Organization.
  3. Is the user an External Client Auditor? → Scope = only the specific project_id list attached to their role assignment, regardless of BU.
  4. Default → Scope = the specific Business Unit(s) the user’s role assignment grants, resolved from the core IAM role-assignment table (never from a client-supplied parameter).
  5. Within whatever scope applies from steps 1–4, individual object action permissions (pqm.review_nc, pqm.verify_nc, etc.) are then checked per-request — scope determines what rows are visible/listed, action permissions determine what the user can do to a row already in view. These are deliberately two separate checks, not one combined check, so a permission bug in one never silently widens the other.

Appendix B. Completeness Gap-Fill — Database, Backend, Frontend, QA/QC & Production Readiness

A full re-audit of v3 against real production requirements surfaced the gaps below. Nothing here contradicts earlier sections — it fills in what a genuine go-live would still be missing.

B.1 Database — Missing Tables & Structural Gaps

New table pqm_checklist / pqm_checklist_item — v3 treats “Checklist Reference” as a free-text field, but real quality inspections run against a structured, versioned checklist (e.g., “Concrete Pour Checklist v2”), not a text note.

pqm_checklist: id, organization_id, name, category_id (FK), version SMALLINT, is_active [+ audit cols]
pqm_checklist_item: id, checklist_id (FK), sequence_order, item_text, is_mandatory BOOLEAN

An NC’s checklist_reference should become a proper FK to pqm_checklist (keep the text field too, for legacy/migrated records that only ever had free text).

New table pqm_drawing_reference — same problem as checklists: drawings get revised, and “Drawing Ref: DWG-104” without a version is ambiguous six months later when DWG-104 Rev C exists.

pqm_drawing_reference: id, organization_id, project_id, drawing_number, revision, file_url, effective_date [+ audit cols]

Link from pqm_non_conformance.drawing_reference (text) to this table via an optional FK once a project’s drawing register exists in the platform; keep the text field as a fallback for tenants without one.

New table pqm_saved_view — Section 18 mentions “saved filter views” in the UI (“My Open NCs”, “Overdue”) but v3 never defines where they persist.

pqm_saved_view: id, user_id (FK), name, filter_json JSONB, is_shared_with_role_id (FK, nullable), is_default BOOLEAN [+ audit cols]

Full-text search: title and description need a Postgres GIN/trigram index (pg_trgm) for “search NCs by keyword” — without this, any search box on the NC list falls back to slow ILIKE scans past a few thousand rows. Add explicitly:

CREATE INDEX idx_pqm_nc_search ON pqm_non_conformance USING GIN (
  to_tsvector('english', title || ' ' || description)
);

Unbounded growth tables: pqm_status_history and pqm_notification_log grow forever with no natural cap. Plan for time-based partitioning (Postgres native partitioning by created_at month/year) once a tenant crosses roughly 1M rows in either table — call this out now in the schema so it’s a planned migration, not a surprise incident later.

Data integrity constraints not yet explicit: add DB-level CHECK constraints, not just application validation, for: target_closure_date >= raised_date, actual_closure_date >= raised_date (when set), status restricted to the enum values from Section 12, current_approval_level >= 0. Application-layer validation can be bypassed by a bad migration script or a direct DB fix during an incident — the DB should refuse bad data regardless of which layer wrote it.

Soft-delete cascade behavior (previously unstated): when an NC is soft-deleted, its child rows (pqm_attachment, pqm_comment, pqm_approval_step, etc.) must be soft-deleted in the same transaction, not left orphaned-but-visible. Define this as a service-layer cascade (nc_service.soft_delete_nc()), not relied on via DB ON DELETE triggers, since soft-delete isn’t a real DELETE.

B.2 Backend — Missing Operational Concerns

API standards not yet defined:

  • Versioning: /api/v1/pqm/... is used throughout, but there’s no stated policy for what happens at v2 — state it now: breaking changes require a new version prefix; additive fields do not.
  • Error response format: define one standard error shape now ({"error_code": "PQM_CLOSURE_GATE_FAILED", "message": "...", "field_errors": {...}}) so frontend error handling isn’t written ad hoc per endpoint.
  • Idempotency keys: mobile/offline sync (Section 20.7) means the same “create NC” or “upload attachment” request may be retried after a dropped connection. Every write endpoint should accept an Idempotency-Key header; the server stores a short-lived record of processed keys and returns the original response for a repeat, rather than creating a duplicate NC.
  • Bulk operations: real Quality Heads will want to bulk-export or bulk-acknowledge a batch of NCs, not click through 40 individually. Add POST /nc/bulk-action/ (actions: export, acknowledge, assign-tag) with a max batch size (e.g., 200) and per-row success/failure reporting in the response, not an all-or-nothing transaction.

Caching: dashboard summary and KPI queries (Section 17) should sit behind a short-TTL cache (Redis, 60–120 seconds) keyed by (organization_id, business_unit_id, user_scope_hash) — without this, a Quality Head’s dashboard re-running full aggregate queries on every page load will be the first thing that falls over under real usage.

Observability: structured logging with a correlation_id per request (propagated through Celery tasks triggered by that request, e.g., notification sends), so a support engineer can trace “why didn’t this notification fire” across the sync request and the async task in one query. Add a /health/ endpoint (DB connectivity, Celery worker liveness, storage connectivity) for uptime monitoring — this is a standard production requirement that’s easy to forget until an incident.

API documentation: auto-generated OpenAPI/Swagger schema from the DRF serializers/viewsets, published at /api/v1/pqm/schema/ — needed for frontend/backend parallel development and for any future external integration (contractor portal, ERP handoff) to have a contract to build against.

Read replica for reporting: once a tenant has meaningful volume, the Reports/Export queries (Section 16) and Dashboard aggregates (Section 17) should read from a replica/read-only connection where available, so a heavy export doesn’t lock rows needed by the live approval workflow.

B.3 Frontend — Missing UX Concerns

Internationalization (i18n) — genuinely important here, not a nice-to-have: site-level users (Site Engineer, Quality Inspector) frequently work primarily in Telugu or Hindi, not English. The frontend should be built with an i18n framework (e.g., react-i18next) from the start, with all user-facing strings in translation keys — retrofitting i18n after the fact across 15+ new components (Section 11.3) is far more expensive than building it in now. Category names, checklist items, and notification templates should also support per-tenant translated variants, not just the UI chrome.

Accessibility: status badges, priority tags, and the safety-critical indicator (Section 18) currently rely partly on color alone — add icon/text redundancy (not color-only) for colorblind users, and ensure the NC form stepper (10.3/11.3) is fully keyboard-navigable, since this is standard enterprise procurement due diligence (WCAG 2.1 AA) that will otherwise surface late as a client requirement.

Print-friendly NC view: site teams frequently need a printed/PDF single-NC report for physical site files or client handover meetings — a dedicated print stylesheet or a “Generate NC Report PDF” button (distinct from the bulk Reports/Export in Section 16) should exist at the NC Detail level.

Bulk-action UI: matching B.2’s bulk API, the NC list needs row-selection checkboxes + a bulk-action toolbar (export selected, tag selected) — otherwise the backend capability has no way to be used.

Loading/error states: explicitly require skeleton loaders (not blank screens) for NC list/detail/dashboard, and a persistent “You’re offline — changes will sync when reconnected” banner tied to the offline-cache behavior in Section 20.7, so field users aren’t left guessing whether their submission actually went through.

Browser/device support matrix: state it explicitly rather than leaving it implied — e.g., latest two versions of Chrome/Edge/Safari, and Android Chrome for field/mobile-responsive use; Internet Explorer not supported.
